Pedagogical Practice

These workshops transform into dynamic laboratories where we dissect the neural anatomy of AI-generated poetry. Students explore how transformer models hallucinate surreal imagery while preserving rhythmic discipline, and design cross-species creative protocols—feeding Shakespearean sonnets into recurrent neural networks, remixing Emily Dickinson through word embedding matrices. Markov chains spark unexpected juxtapositions, sentiment analysis tools map emotional quantum leaps in verse, and learners cultivate meta-skills for dialoguing with algorithmic collaborators.

Technical Deconstruction

This analytical framework decodes AI's creative processes by reverse-engineering how language models transform semantic structures into poetic output. Focusing on key elements that govern machine-aesthetic synthesis, we examine attention mechanisms orchestrating rhyme recognition, latent space walks generating surreal image juxtapositions, temperature parameters modulating linguistic unpredictability for defamiliarization effects, and prompt engineering evolving as a computational poetics syntax. These systematically studied mechanisms are formalized into transferrable creative protocols, bridging neural architectures' technical operations with intentional human-AI artistic collaboration.

Artistic Philosophy

This journey transcends tool-building—it's a dual cognitive revolution. While training AI to comprehend humanity's poetic spectrum, we harness machine "alienness" to bypass creative blind spots. When neural networks output Rorschach-like digital haikus, they become cybernetic prisms refracting the human condition. Our workshops prototype a future where poet-programmers fluent in both iambic parameters and machine learning parameters co-author literature. The emerging paradigm isn't human versus machine, but a symbiotic ecosystem where silicon-based computation and carbon-based inspiration achieve quantum entanglement—and our classroom is writing its foundational code.
